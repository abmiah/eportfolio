Title:
The Ethics of Data Science Companies Influencing or Manipulating an Audience in a Political Election.

Abstract: {250 words}
This will provide a quick summary of the research, methods, and findings.

Introduction: {1200 words}
This introduction section will outline the research problem, context, aims, and research question to justify the necessity of the research. Additionally, this section includes data sets and possible strategies to avoid misleading information, which will be presented in a code format.

Background: {2800 words} (Critical Review of Literature)
In this section, the current literature on the topic will be reviewed, how the audience was manipulated, and the techniques used to influence them. Mention any ethical framework. A case study on Cambridge Analytica should also mention any gaps within the research.

Ethical and Professional Considerations: {1500 words}
This section will examine the BCS Code of Conduct, which promotes integrity and is an ethical guideline for IT professionals. It will also compare it to other codes of practice, such as ACM and IEEE, while examining ethical theories such as utilitarianism, deontology, and virtue ethics. Additionally, this area should explore any legal and social implications.

Main Body: {4000 words}
This section presents the main findings from analysing the case studies, data, or relevant documentation. Further discussion will examine how data science techniques can sway or manipulate audiences, evaluating the ethical boundaries and real-world impacts. Finally, it will show integrated technical code, summarised with a screenshot of the code linked to GitHub.

Evaluation : {1200 words}
Evaluate the strengths and weaknesses of the research and technical solution. Consider aspects like validity, reliability, and possible biases.

Learning or reflections: {800 words}
Outline critically any personal learning experiences, challenges faced during the research, skills gained, and the implications for future work.

Conclusion: {750 words}
Summary of key findings, addressing the research question, and providing recommendations for policy, practice, and future research.

Total word count of 12500, allowing an additional 500 words for extra leeway.
