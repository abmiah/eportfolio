Working Title:

Example 1:
The Ethics of Data Science Companies Influencing or Manipulating Audiences on the Social Media Platform, Facebook.

Example 2:
The Ethical Implications of Data Science Institutions in Manipulating and Influencing Audiences on the Social Media Platform Facebook.

Both titles convey the same idea, albeit with different phrasing.

Abstract: {650 words}(1/2-page overview of your project.)
This will provide an overview of the paper, summarising the research, problem, methodology used, and highlighting any findings and their implications. .

Introduction: {1300 words} (To the Research Problem and Question)
This introduction section will outline the research problem, context, aims, and research question to justify the necessity of the research and the ethical concerns of manipulating Facebook users. The research question and its objective are aimed at avoiding misleading information that is presented in a code format.

Background: {2600 words} (Critical Review of Literature)
This section will review the current literature on the topic, explore how the audience was manipulated on Facebook, and examine the techniques used to influence them. It will also address the ethical perceptions of social media research and any data utilised. This includes an investigation into privacy and misinformation within the digital sphere. Furthermore, a case study on Cambridge Analytica should highlight any gaps in existing research.

Ethical and Professional Considerations: {1500 words}
This section will examine the BCS Code of Conduct, which promotes integrity and serves as an ethical guideline for IT professionals regarding social media data usage, such as GDPR compliance and ESRC guidance. It will also explore ethical theories such as utilitarianism, deontology, and virtue ethics. Furthermore, this area should investigate any legal and social implications concerning the duty of care within the Facebook environment, along with the professional practices and responsibilities that data companies must adhere to.

Main Body: {4000 words}
This section presents the main findings from analysing the case studies, data, or relevant documentation. Further discussion will examine how data science techniques can sway or manipulate audiences, evaluating the ethical boundaries and real-world impacts. This will also look into data protection from a cybersecurity viewpoint. Finally, it will show integrated technical code, summarised with a screenshot of the code linked to GitHub.

Evaluation : {1200 words}
Evaluate the research and technical solution's strengths and weaknesses. Consider aspects such as validity, reliability, and potential biases, including any study limitations and the effectiveness of current ethical guidelines.

Learning or reflections: {650 words}
Outline critically any personal learning experiences, challenges faced during the research, skills gained, and the implications for future work.

Conclusion: {750 words}
Summary of key findings, addressing the research question, and providing recommendations for policy, practice, and future research.

Total word count of 12650, allowing an additional 350 words for extra leeway.
