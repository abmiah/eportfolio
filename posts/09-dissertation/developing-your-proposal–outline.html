Activity - Unit 1

Sunday, 11 May 2025, 15:33 PM

Research area and working title:

Example 1:
The Ethics of Data Science Companies Influencing or Manipulating Audiences on the Social Media Platform, Facebook.

Example 2:
The Ethical Implications of Data Science Institutions in Manipulating and Influencing Audiences on the Social Media Platform Facebook.

Both titles express the same concept; they have just been reworded.

Proposed Research Problem and Research Question:
This will examine the ethical aspects of data science company Cambridge Analytica, which harbours vast quantities of user data to influence and sway them toward a particular agenda. This will investigate the case of Cambridge Analytica and data abuse, aligning with the CyBOKframework regarding human factors, organisational, and regulatory aspects. This will discuss the ethical, legal, and social consequences of the company’s practices regarding Facebook.

Proposed Aims and Objectives:
The objective is to explore how specific agendas can readily sway individuals through the collection or dependence on user data and how Facebook has become an instrument for promoting misinformation. Do companies such as Facebook hold any responsibility for influencing users towards a misinformation agenda, and do they consider the security of users' data when third-party applications access it?

Proposed Research Design:
This will examine the infamous Cambridge Analytica and how it used data to influence its alleged clients towards a particular view. It will explore the platforms utilised and various data points collected to gather users’ information to advance the client's agenda.

Artefact(s) that can be created:
The main artefact will display technical code for mitigation while verifying if these campaigns are legitimate by searching through credible sources.
