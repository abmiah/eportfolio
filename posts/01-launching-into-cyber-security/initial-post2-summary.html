Summary Post - Security technology

Collaborative Learning Discussion - Unit 8

Monday, 2 October 2023, 12:33 AM

The forum discussed the implementation of two security technologies: Firewall and antivirus protection. These are essential forms of protection for small and medium-sized businesses (National Cyber Security Centre, 2017).

Jeff Mogul created the packet-filtering Firewall in 1988, which checks incoming and outgoing packets based on predetermined rules. (Liang & Kim, 2022; kaspersky.com, 2021). Firewalls were further developed by Gil Shwaed in 1993, who pioneered the modern computer Firewall with Stateful Inspection. The software decides which network packets are allowed through to the Firewall; this is useful for protocols and can accommodate the User Datagram Protocol (UDP) (Sheldon, 2021; Computerweekly.com, 2020).

Modern operating systems have standard built-in Firewalls that monitor incoming and outgoing traffic. In 2009, Firewall technology evolved to Next-Generation Firewalls (NGFW) and Application-Based Firewall. (Liang & Kim, 2022; Cisco.com. 2019).

Mohammed Younus highlighted that cybercriminals prowl online, searching for network systems without essential Firewall protection and further supporting one argument of activating the basic in-built Firewall. However, SMBs and SMEs require a more robust firewall to protect multiple networks. Wiley Finch pointed out the limitations of standard Firewalls. Kaur et al., (2014) discussed six advantages and disadvantages of traditional Firewalls; further research (Liang & Kim, 2022) supports that the NGFW tackles many drawbacks, like packet inspection, which cannot protect against application-layered attacks. NGFWs, by contrast, offer higher protection without the loss of performance.

Malware attacks can still happen even with a sophisticated firewall. Malware can enter systems through various avenues. One can lower the risk using vigorous antivirus software designed to scan computer systems, identifying and isolating irregular codes. Wiley Finch stated that having an Endpoint Detection and Response (EDR) would add another layer of protection, which agrees with Finch's statement. According to ibm.com (n.d.) EDR provides security at the endpoints of users, IT systems or devices against cyber threats that may go past the antivirus. EDR works by continuously collecting and analysing data in real-time to detect and respond to any potential cyber threat.
