Case Study Review: Smart Usage

Blog post - Unit 1

Friday, 14 July 2024, 20:30 PM

A brief overview on the case study titled “Can serious gaming tactics bolster spear-phishing and phishing resilience? : Securing the human hacking in Information Security.”

The article introduces 'PhishDefend,' a series of games that serve as evidence of the power of gamification in cybersecurity education. The game educates people about phishing attacks and social engineering techniques and enhances cybersecurity awareness and skills through an interactive learning experience. It follows a structured process, which includes steps such as identification, analysis, and response to phishing attacks, and incorporates various card types, each representing a different type of phishing attack or social engineering technique. 'PhishDefend' is more than just a game; it is a potent tool that effectively combines gamification, collaborative learning, and cybersecurity knowledge, teaching confidence in its ability to strengthen cybersecurity resilience. Its effectiveness in improving cybersecurity skills is well-documented, making it a valuable resource in cybersecurity education.

The article emphasises human vulnerability as the weakest point regarding phishing attacks, which can result in breaches and lead to reputational and financial damages for organisations. Spotting phishing emails is imperative, as cybercriminals employ deceptive messages and emails that exploit human emotions and triggers to expose sensitive data.

Research papers have shown that interventions through game-based approaches knowingly enhance the identification of fraudulent emails by promoting caution when faced with requests for sensitive data. This strengthens resistance to social engineering tactics, a crucial skill in the cybersecurity landscape.
