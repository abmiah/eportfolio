Quantitative Risk Modelling

Unit 8 Seminar

Tuesday, 19 March 2024, 11:53 PM

Question:
How do Goerlandt et al. (2017) suggest that the validity of QRA approaches can be validated? What did they posit was the most effective approach?

Summary:
The paper by Goerlandt et al. (2017) titled "Validity and Validation of Safety-Related Quantitative Risk Analysis: A Review" presents a comprehensive analysis of the efficacy and validation mechanisms of Quantitative Risk Analysis (QRA) in safety-critical contexts. The key findings of their research highlight the significance of comprehensive validation processes and quality assurance measures in ensuring the dependability of safety-related quantitative risk analysis techniques.

Validation Approaches
The document expounds upon several approaches to ascertain the pragmatic validity of a risk analysis, encompassing benchmark exercises, reality checks, independent peer reviews, and quality assurance methods.

Reality Checks
The study aimed to assess the efficacy of various hazard identification techniques, including HAZOP (hazard and operability study), AEA (action error analysis), FMEA (failure mode and effect analysis), and MORT (management oversight and risk tree ), in identifying factors contributing to accidents. To achieve this, the methods were compared with accident descriptions.

Quality Assurance
The present discourse underscores the criticality of evaluating the quality of risk analysis, with particular emphasis on comprehensive documentation and adaptable assessment methodologies. To this end, the concept of quality control in risk analysis validation is introduced.

Peer Review Process
This study underscores the criticality of independent peer review in decision-making processes that involve quantitative risk analysis (QRA). It sheds light on the challenges of ensuring reliability across different review teams and proposes adopting standard reporting formats and comprehensive evaluation guidance.

Risk Analysis Validation Criteria
The proposed criteria for validating risk analysis are crucial. They encompass various aspects, including scope and objectives, hazard identification, risk assessment, risk control options, and stakeholder feedback. Adhering to these criteria is fundamental to ensuring the completeness and accuracy of the risk analysis process. It is through these criteria that the risk analysis can be validated, ensuring its reliability and validity.

This paper underscores the significance of implementing comprehensive validation processes and quality assurance measures to ensure the dependability of safety-related quantitative risk analysis techniques. It stresses the need for a meticulous and systematic approach to risk analysis, which involves a thorough examination and critical evaluation of underlying assumptions and methodologies. Additionally, it emphasizes the importance of adhering to established standards and guidelines for conducting risk assessments to ensure the consistency and accuracy of results.

Conclusion
The author suggests that the validity of Quantitative Risk Analysis (QRA) approaches can be validated through various methods, including benchmark exercises, independent peer reviews, and quality assurance processes. However, the most effective method proposed by Suokas (1985) is the reality check. This method involves comparing the results of a specific QRA with the operating experience of corresponding systems or activities. By validating the analysis through comparisons with real-world accidents and disturbances, insights can be gained into the effectiveness and accuracy of the risk analysis model.
