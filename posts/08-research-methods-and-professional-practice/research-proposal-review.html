Research Proposal Review

Activity - Unit 3

Thursday, 20 February 2025, 11:37 PM

  • Which of the methods described in this week's reading would you think would suit your purpose?

The methodology employed in this research will primarily take a deductive approach, utilising quantitative research methods. This framework aligns with the literature review on the topic titled “Financial Impact of Ransomware and Malware in the Health Sector in the UK.”

Deductive research fundamentally embraces a scientific methodology in which a systematic series of empirical tests is formulated to determine the veracity of the proposed theory (Edgar and Manz, 2017, pp.95–130). Quantitative analysis employs empirical data to assess a particular phenomenon, facilitating the formulation of conclusions drawn from the analysed data set (Fleetwood, 2018). This research approach is grounded in empirical evidence, which enhances decision-making processes, as the data points are typically distinct, rigorously defined, and systematically organised (Dudovskiy, 2009).

  • Which data collection methods would you consider using?

Although the primary research relies on secondary data, the findings will illustrate the financial impact of cyber attacks on the UK healthcare system. This data will represent the losses that organisations have sustained, and since the National Health Service (NHS) is a government-owned entity, the figures will be accurate based on what the government decides to publish to the public. Should a questionnaire be proposed, the methodology employed will primarily focus on the use of closed-ended questions, including dichotomous responses such as “yes” or "no,’ along with multiple-choice options.

  • Which required skills will you need to have or develop for the chosen project?

In the context of necessary skill sets, it is imperative for individuals to familiarize themselves with data analysis; not exclusively at an advanced level of computer science but rather possessing a robust working knowledge of Microsoft Excel. Furthermore, a comprehension of how to design quantitative research through a systematically structured set of questions is essential.
