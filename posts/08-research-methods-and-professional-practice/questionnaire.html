Wiki Activity: Questionnaires

Activity - Unit 5

Sunday, 23 February 2025, 02:17 AM

In the course of this study, I examined a questionnaire provided by Ofsted that addressed children in social care. This questionnaire was administered in 2024, and the subsequent findings are as follows:

The survey generated a total of 43,777 responses from a diverse array of audience categories, with a noteworthy contribution of 6,995 responses from children. While this overall total reflects a slight decrease from the preceding year's total of 45,655 responses, it is important to highlight an increase in submissions from the children's demographic, which rose from 6,614 responses in the prior year.

Some of the findings include the following:

Here are some of the key findings from the survey: the results of a survey conducted among various groups of children indicate consistent sentiments relative to the previous year. A significant majority of participants expressed a positive perception regarding their living conditions and social environment, with nearly all respondents indicating a sense of safety within their home environments. Nonetheless, an increased proportion of children residing in residential special schools conveyed feelings of insecurity, illustrating a decline in perceived safety when compared to earlier years. Moreover, these children reported diminished social cohesion, even though they spent time with individuals deemed significant to them. In contrast, children living in supported accommodation reported feeling safe at home, albeit with less engagement with pivotal individuals. Conversely, those in foster care demonstrated enhanced relational dynamics with their peers and exhibited greater interaction with significant others than observed in previous assessments.

General observation of the questionnaire:

It is imperative to acknowledge that the inquiries posed are specifically directed at children, a demographic that may include individuals deemed vulnerable and exhibiting varied levels of social anxiety. Consequently, it is essential to articulate these questions in a manner that ensures children's comprehension, thereby eschewing unnecessary complexity. This consideration is particularly salient, given that children residing in care facilities may lack exposure to a stable and consistent educational environment, thereby emphasising the necessity for a questionnaire design that is both accessible and developmentally appropriate.

The questions are meticulously structured to underscore the needs and well-being of children, thereby aligning with the overarching objective of the questionnaire. Subsequent enhancements will entail the incorporation of demographic variables, including age, nationality, gender, and the geographic location of caregivers. Such inquiries will facilitate the identification of specific child populations frequently in care as well as the regions in which their caregivers are predominantly situated.
