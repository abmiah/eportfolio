Summary - Cybercrime and Organised Crime

Collaborative Learning Discussion - Unit 4

Friday, 23 August 2024, 9:24 PM

For this discussion forum, one had to read an extract from Tropina (2010) titled Cybercrime and Organised Crime; my post discusses how the digital revolution has transformed criminal activities, making cybercrime an attractive option for organised crime groups due to its high returns and low risk. This has created challenges for law enforcement, as the internet's privacy and territorial boundaries complicate investigations. Cybercrime led by organised criminal groups is primarily driven by financial motives and often involves collaboration with criminal syndicates and those with technical knowledge. In some countries, addressing international cybercrimes is difficult due to inadequate legislation and tools. Organisations such as Interpol are crucial in partnering with public and private sectors to combat cybercrime globally.

Daniel Malok has postulated that the proliferation of cybercrime can be attributed to certain countries' lack of regulatory frameworks. Consequently, cybercrime has evolved into a viable business model, with cybercriminals constantly leveraging new technologies to circumvent detection and legal repercussions, according to Deora and Chudasama (2021). Additionally, Bandler and Merzon (2020, p.62) advocate for the establishment of a framework for law enforcement to investigate cybercrime, facilitating collaboration between the agency and the private sector. This initiative holds the potential to enhance their working relationship.

Mohammed Younes points out that, according to Tropina (2020), the internet's inherently borderless nature poses challenges for law enforcement in addressing cybercrime due to jurisdictional limitations.

Syed Imran Ali has observed that cryptocurrency has introduced additional complexity, as tracing transactions adds a layer of intricacy for law enforcement. According to EUROPOL (2021), there has been an observed surge in the adoption of cryptocurrency as a means of payment by criminal elements. Nevertheless, the utilisation of digital currency is encumbered by various constraints, which may discourage organised criminal groups from considering it a viable option for long-term investment.
